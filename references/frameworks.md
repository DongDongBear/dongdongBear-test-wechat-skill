# Article Frameworks

> 5 frameworks with execution-level guidance. Framework choice determines structure;
> CRAFT (see writing-guide.md) determines quality. A mediocre framework well-executed
> beats a perfect framework with flat writing.

---

## Quick Selection Matrix

| Signal in Topic | Framework | Why |
|----------------|-----------|-----|
| Reader has a clear, specific pain point | Pain-Point | Direct value, highest engagement baseline |
| Compelling person + turning point exists | Story | Strongest completion rate, hardest to execute |
| 5+ genuinely useful, non-obvious items | Listicle | Most shareable, highest risk of feeling thin |
| Two views that smart people genuinely debate | Comparison | Drives comments, needs real balance |
| Breaking news or major change | Hot Take | Highest initial CTR, shortest shelf life (24-72h) |
| Uncertain | Pain-Point | Most forgiving framework |

## 1. Pain-Point (痛点型)

**Best for:** Reader has tried and failed to solve a problem.
**Risk:** Degrades into generic advice if the pain isn't specific enough.
**Rating:** Most reliable framework. Works for ~60% of topics.

### Opening — The Mirror (50–100 words)

Drop the reader into a scene where they recognize themselves. NOT "很多人都有这个问题." A specific moment:

> "周五下午四点，你盯着还没写完的周报，微信群里已经开始约晚饭了。你知道这个报告两小时就能写完，但你已经拖了三天——不是不会写，是根本不想打开那个Word文档。"

**Execution key:** The reader must think "this is literally me" within 3 sentences. If you can't write a scene that specific, the pain point isn't concrete enough — go back to topic selection.

### Validation — "You're Not Alone" (100–200 words)

Prove this pain is widespread using CONCRETE evidence:
- Name real numbers: "我做了个小调查，47个人里有39个说..."
- Reference identifiable scenarios: "前两天在一个产品经理群里，有人发了张截图..."
- Social proof: quote a real comment, DM, or conversation

**Do NOT:** cite vague statistics ("research shows...") or use "很多人" / "大部分人" without specifics.

### Solution — The Ladder (400–800 words)

3-5 progressive layers. Each feels like a LEVEL UP, not an independent tip.

**Critical execution:**
- Lead with the most **counterintuitive** solution first (earns trust)
- Each solution must pass the "you can try this RIGHT NOW" test
- VARY depth dramatically: one solution gets 3 sentences, another gets a full paragraph with a real example
- Connect layers: "做到了上面这个之后，你会发现一个新问题..."

### Proof — Evidence It Works (100–200 words)

Before/after comparison with specific outcomes:
> "用了这个方法的第一周，周报从平均拖3天变成了周五中午前搞定。不是因为变勤快了，是因为发现原来的流程里有个隐形的摩擦点。"

### Close — One Punch + Engagement (50 words)

One punchy summary sentence + ask for their experience:
> "说白了就一个字：别跟惰性硬刚，绕过去。你是怎么对付拖延的？"

### Golden Quote Placement
- End of validation section (empathy peak)
- After the most counterintuitive solution (insight peak)

## 2. Story (故事型)

**Best for:** A real person with a real turning point and a transferable insight.
**Risk:** Becomes a generic biography if the story lacks genuine surprise.
**Rating:** Strongest completion rate when executed well. Requires quality source material.

### Hook — The Flash-Forward (30–50 words)

Start at the most dramatic moment. Make the reader NEED to know what happened:

> "2019年，张一鸣做了一个决定，公司里90%的人都觉得他疯了。三年后，这个决定值1000亿美元。"

**Execution key:** The hook must create a specific question ("what was the decision?") that the reader can't ignore.

### Context — Building Stakes (150–300 words)

Make the reader care about the person BEFORE the turning point:
- Show the constraints that made the situation seem impossible
- Build tension through specifics: "所有人都说应该选A。他也这么觉得。但有一个细节让他犹豫了。"
- Give the protagonist a recognizable human quality (ambition, fear, stubbornness)

**Do NOT** info-dump background. Reveal context through the narrative.

### Turn — The Moment (200–400 words)

This is the climax. SLOW DOWN the pacing here:
- Use sensory details — what did the room look like? What was the weather?
- Show internal conflict, not just the external action
- The decision must feel EARNED — not lucky, not obvious in hindsight
- If the reader can predict the turn from the hook, you've failed

### Insight — What This Means For You (200–300 words)

Extract the transferable lesson WITHOUT moralizing:
- Frame as "Here's what I took from this..." not "This teaches us that..."
- The insight should SURPRISE — if predictable from the hook, dig deeper
- Bridge to the reader: "你可能没有千亿的决定要做，但每个人都有过那种..."

### Close — The Echo (50–100 words)

Return to a universal truth + leave an open question:
> "故事讲完了。但真正让我想了很久的不是结果，而是那个犹豫的瞬间。你上一次在所有人都说A的时候，选了B，是什么时候？"

### Golden Quote Placement
- Maximum tension moment before the turn
- The single most transferable insight sentence

## 3. Listicle (清单型)

**Best for:** Methods, tools, techniques — where you have 5+ genuinely valuable, non-obvious items.
**Risk:** Reads like a Wikipedia list if items lack depth, surprise, or personality.
**Rating:** Easiest to share (inherently scannable), highest risk of feeling superficial.

### Promise — The Contract (50–80 words)

Be specific about what you're delivering AND qualify it:

> "下面这8个工具，是我花了半年时间一个一个试出来的。不是'值得一试'——是'试了之后你会骂为什么没早发现'的那种。每个我都附了具体使用场景和踩过的坑。"

**Execution key:** The promise must be specific enough that the reader can judge if you kept it.

### Items — Deep, Not Wide (800–1,500 words total)

Each item needs ALL of these:
1. **What it is** (1 sentence — name and core function)
2. **Why it matters** / the specific scenario where it shines (2-3 sentences)
3. **Concrete example or use case** from your own experience (2-3 sentences)
4. **The catch / limitation / gotcha** (1-2 sentences — this builds trust)

**CRITICAL: Vary the depth.** Item 1 gets 200 words. Item 2 gets 80. Item 3 gets 150. Uniform length = reader boredom = drop-off.

**Do NOT:** just describe each item. You must show you've USED it and have an OPINION.

### Star Pick + Bonus (100–150 words)

> "如果你只试一个，选 #3。原因很简单：门槛最低但效果最明显。另外附赠一个没列进去的..."

### Golden Quote Placement
- One punchy summary per item (quotable and shareable)
- The star pick rationale

## 4. Comparison (对比型)

**Best for:** Two options that people genuinely debate. A real choice with real trade-offs.
**Risk:** Degrades into a pro/con list if you don't take a position.
**Rating:** Strong comment driver. Requires genuine balance — if one option is clearly worse, use a different framework.

### The Dilemma (50–100 words)

Frame as a decision the reader is CURRENTLY facing:

> "最近半年，我被问得最多的问题：'该学Python还是JavaScript？'这个问题的答案不是'看情况'——是大多数人问错了问题。"

**Execution key:** If possible, reframe the question itself. This is the highest-value move.

### Deep Dive A (300–500 words)

- Strengths with specific evidence (not "it's good at X")
- Weaknesses that only someone with experience would know
- The ideal user profile: "If you are [specific person] in [specific situation], this is your answer."

### Deep Dive B (300–500 words)

Same depth. BOTH options must feel genuinely viable. If B is clearly weaker, you don't have a comparison — you have a recommendation.

### The Table

| Dimension | Option A | Option B |

Visual, scannable, maximum 6 rows. Use specific descriptors, not just "good/bad."

### Your Take + Invitation (100–150 words)

State your actual position with a PERSONAL reason:
> "如果是我，我选A。原因只有一个：[specific personal reason]。但我知道至少三种情况下B更好。你呢？"

**Do NOT:** end with "it depends on your situation" — that's a non-answer.

### Golden Quote Placement
- The reframe of the original question
- Your verdict sentence

## 5. Hot Take (热点解读型)

**Best for:** Breaking news, industry shifts, policy changes everyone's discussing.
**Risk:** You're late (>72 hours), or you're just adding noise without genuine insight.
**Rating:** Highest initial CTR. Shortest shelf life. Only write this if you have something others haven't said.

### News Flash (50–100 words)

What happened, stripped to bare facts. No opinion yet:
> "昨天下午，OpenAI宣布了GPT-5。一小时内全网刷屏。但如果你只看了标题，你可能错过了发布会上最重要的30秒。"

### Surface Layer — What Everyone Sees (100–200 words)

The mainstream narrative, presented fairly. The reader needs to know you understand the common view before you challenge it.

### Deep Layer — What You See (300–500 words)

This is your VALUE. The insight others missed.

**Requirements:**
- At least ONE piece of information or perspective not in mainstream coverage
- Frame as: "但如果你仔细看...", "有个细节没人提到...", "这让我想到一个更大的问题..."
- Support with evidence or reasoning — not just assertion

### Impact Forecast — 3-5 Specific Predictions (200–400 words)

What this means for the reader's world:
- Each prediction must be specific and testable (not "will change everything")
- Order from "affects you this week" → "affects you this year"
- For each: explain the mechanism, not just the prediction

### Action Items (50–100 words)

Concrete, immediate:
> "今天你可以做两件事：1. [具体行动] 2. [具体行动]。如果只做一件，做第一个。"

### Golden Quote Placement
- Core deep-layer insight
- Boldest impact prediction

## Framework Combination

For complex topics, combine two frameworks. Use ONE for overall structure, embed elements of the other:

| Combination | How It Works |
|-------------|-------------|
| Story + Pain-Point | Open with a story, pivot to solutions at the turn |
| Hot Take + Comparison | News breaks → compare two response strategies |
| Pain-Point + Listicle | Frame the pain → deliver solutions as a curated list |
| Story + Hot Take | A person's experience illustrates a broader trend |

---

## Framework Selection Gotchas

**"Everything is a pain point":** If you default to Pain-Point for every article, your content becomes predictable. Force yourself to use Story or Comparison at least once every 3 articles.

**"The story without a turn":** If your Story framework doesn't have a genuine surprise or reversal, switch to Pain-Point. A flat story is worse than no story.

**"The fake comparison":** If one option is obviously better, you don't have a comparison article. Either find genuine strengths in the "weaker" option or switch frameworks.

**"The stale hot take":** If the news is >48 hours old and you don't have a genuinely unique angle, don't write it. A late, generic hot take damages credibility.
